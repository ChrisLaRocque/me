Web Tech

I've been using HTML every day since 2015. I enjoy creating clean, readable, useful markup. I compulsively format and comment. While it seems most folks like robust tools that make their job easier (myself included), there's been plenty of times where my work needs to end up being 'turned in' as a single HTML file.

I'm a big flexbox fan (though grid does the job just fine) and am eternally grateful to css-tricks.com/ for showing me better ways to solve the problems I encounter.

8 out of 10

If I love CSS, I am in love with Sass. I've been lucky enough to work with fantastic developers/designers who illustrated just how useful Sass' abilities when trying to build a consistent brand experience on the web.


While BEM isn't specific to Sass, Sass' functionality and syntax style combined with BEM's naming convention allowed the team at Spartan Race to quickly iterate and modify our design components where needed.

7 out of 10

My typical vanilla JS use breaks down into either site behavior/interactions or dealing with validating and then submitting form data.


Behavior/interaction typically includes querying the document for the presence of specific elements or user interaction; then modifying their styles, animations, and visibility. My pages for Brightcove and Spartan describe each company's form needs in depth.

6 out of 10

My React experience has consisted largely of creating functional components to help transition brightcove.com from their old stack to their new. Largely I took design mocks in Figma or XD and created React components to match that design. From there we built out page templates constructed of these components to be built by Gatsby.

4 out of 10

My Vue experience is largely similar to my React experience (transitioning pre-existing content in Contentful to new front end, building Vue components to match content model + provided design mocks. Due to the nature of my role and the division of labor on the Spartan team at the time my exposure to view was admittedly limited and at this point, outdated.

5 out of 10

I've used GraphQL hand-in-hand with Gatsby to get data from Contentful to the React components being built for brightcove.com. I've primarily created queries for relevant content types in Contentful to which ever component or page I'm asked to build. GraphiQL is a tool you can use with Gatsby that makes it very easy to generate queries.

9 out of 10

I don't know if I'm allowed to consider myself an expert in Contentful, but I love the product and I love talking about it. I've been using Contentful for almost 5 years now and have learned its ins and outs. I feel it's both developer and end-user friendly, and love trying to find creative ways Contentful can solve content and localization challenges. My work has often included creating documentation and videos to bring people up to speed on making site updates.
